FAQ

These are some of the most common questions people ask about web development, SEO, SEM, and GEO.

Should web development and SEO be handled separately or together?
It is usually better to plan them together. Site structure, content organization, and technical foundations all directly affect SEO performance. When search and conversion logic are considered during the build stage, you avoid a lot of unnecessary rework later.
How should SEO and SEM work together?
SEO is stronger for long-term organic growth, while SEM is stronger for immediate high-intent acquisition. In most cases, the more effective approach is to use SEM for short-term goals while using SEO to build long-term content strength and organic visibility.
What is the difference between GEO and traditional SEO?
GEO focuses more on brand visibility in generative search, AI answer engines, and newer retrieval environments. Beyond page optimization itself, it also emphasizes identity signals, case evidence, FAQs, content consistency, and trustworthiness.
I already have a website. Do I need to rebuild it?
Not necessarily. Many projects do not require a full rebuild. The better first step is to determine whether the issue is positioning, structure, content, search foundations, or landing page conversion, and then decide whether partial optimization or a full upgrade makes more sense.
