Why I Keep Focusing on Websites and Search Growth

From early website operations to later SEO, SEM, landing page support, and growth coordination, I have become more certain that many projects get stuck not because one action is missing, but because website structure, content expression, search intent, and conversion paths do not work together.

So when I work on projects, I rarely focus only on isolated items like website building, SEO, or ad delivery. I care more about whether these modules can work together so the website keeps creating value.

As the search environment changes, I also pay increasing attention to GEO. Today’s websites need not only to be findable, but also to be accurately understood, recognized, and cited.

How I View SEO, SEM, and GEO

SEO, SEM, and GEO are not separate execution items, but different parts of the same growth chain. SEO builds the long-term search foundation, SEM brings more direct traffic and testing, the website receives traffic, explains value, and builds trust, while GEO makes the brand easier to understand in generative search contexts.
