FAQ

Questions You May Care About Before Starting Cooperation

The questions below can help you quickly judge whether I am suitable for your corporate website, independent site planning, SEO, SEM, GEO, or deployment optimization project.

FAQ

What areas do you mainly support?

I mainly provide website planning, website implementation, SEO optimization, Google Ads landing page support, GEO content optimization, server deployment, and long-term operations support.

Do you only build websites or run ads?

No. A website should not only be built or used to receive traffic; it should also support brand expression, search visibility, ad landing, user trust, and long-term operation.

How do technology choices differ between overseas independent sites and corporate websites?

Overseas independent sites choose among WordPress, Shopify, and other solutions based on project goals; corporate websites usually focus more on content management, search optimization, promotion support, and maintenance efficiency.

Do you also provide deployment environment and technical support?

Yes. I can help handle domains, servers, runtime environments, basic configuration, performance optimization, and launch deployment so the website has a runnable, maintainable, and optimizable foundation.

Why include GEO in website planning early?

Because generative search is changing how users discover brands. GEO does not replace SEO; it further optimizes page structure, identity information, case evidence, FAQs, and content consistency.

Can I contact you without a clear plan?

Yes. Many projects start with only a general direction. I can first help sort out a more suitable website development and optimization plan based on your business and goals.

How does cooperation usually start?

Usually, I first understand the business model, target customers, website purpose, content foundation, and promotion needs, then organize the direction, propose a plan, and move forward step by step.

How can I contact you?

You can contact me by phone, email, Telegram, or the contact form, and briefly describe the most important problem you want to solve.
